⚡
Every Ad Must Follow These Rules
Memorise These
▼
1. Target emotions — emotion comes first, always
2. There must be a promise
3. There must be proof
4. Zero confusion — keep it simple
5. They must sell (we are a performance agency)
6. They must entertain — no dull moments
7. They must be memorable
8. Creativity wins
9. Assumptions are the death of ads
10. Show the product — ideally in first 2 seconds

People buy because of emotion, not logic. Logic comes in when they justify the purchase. If you can't name the emotion or mindstate your ad targets, scrap it and start again.

Phase 1
Research
The cornerstone of all great advertising. Without research, no ad ideas.
🤖
1A — AI-Assisted Research (Prompt Chain)
AI Can Own → Strategist Reviews
▼

Run the 6-prompt research chain to build an initial intelligence base. This gives you a fast foundation — but it is not a replacement for manual research.

  • Prompt 1: Market Research — competitive landscape, market size, trends
  • Prompt 2: Psychographic Research — deep emotional drivers, fears, aspirations
  • Prompt 3: Unique Mechanism Ideation — differentiated product positioning
  • Prompt 4: Review Mining (with CSV) — structured extraction from customer reviews
  • Prompt 5: Winning Video Analysis — breakdown of top-performing ads
  • Prompt 6: Unified Report — synthesise all prompts into one strategy document

Output: A unified research document that serves as the base layer for all creative work.

📊
1B — Review Mining
AI Can Own
▼

Pull reviews from the website, Amazon, Trustpilot — everywhere you can find them. Export as CSVs and run through the AI review mining process.

What it extracts: Pain points, desired outcomes, purchase prompts, UVPs, features/benefits, emotional language, golden one-liners, before/after stories, failed solutions.

Think of this as having an AI detective — it gives you a rapid snapshot of how customers talk, what they care about, and what moves them. This becomes your base for manual research.

Output: Review Mining Doc → stored in Google Doc / company drive.

🔍
1C — Product Deep Dive
Strategist
▼

Understand the product inside out. You can't sell what you don't understand.

  • How does the product actually work?
  • What are its ingredients / components?
  • Features → Benefits → Desires (map the chain)
  • What is unique vs. competitors? What's the mechanism?
  • What problems does it solve? For whom?
  • USPs that can become ad claims
🕵️
1D — Manual Research (The Deep Work)
Strategist — This Is Where the Gold Is
▼

This is where the best ad ideas come from. AI gives you the base; manual research gives you the stories, formats, and raw human truth that make ads hit.

Reddit Deep Dives
  • Search pain points directly ("dry skin", "can't sleep", "dog pulling on leash")
  • Find stories you can recreate in ads
  • "I want" statements (desires), "I tried X" (experiences), "I feel X" (emotions)
  • "I always/never do X" (behaviors)
  • Screenshot and save golden nuggets
TikTok & YouTube Shorts
  • Find top-performing organic content in the niche
  • Read the comments — pain points, desires, language patterns
  • Note what formats and content styles the audience engages with
  • These give you both stories AND formats to work with
Competitor & Ad Library Research
  • Meta Ad Library, Kalodata — what are competitors running?
  • Dissect their messaging, hooks, formats, which avatars they target
  • What are they missing? (Format gaps, avatar gaps, awareness level gaps)
  • What are boring brands doing that makes their ads pop?
  • Look at indirect competitors too
Long-Form YouTube & Niche Content
  • What desires do niche creators discuss?
  • What pain points come up naturally?
  • What mechanisms/promises are made?
  • How do they build authority?
Trend & Seasonality Research
  • Google Trends: product + emotional state keywords
  • Seasonality plays (e.g. "Candles" + "Self Care" in winter)
  • Keep your finger on the pulse — always searching for new stories and untapped angles
📈
1E — Ad Account Audit & Cross-Analysis
AI-Assisted — Strategist Interprets
▼

Analyse the ad account (Adnova for winners, Facebook for losers). This is also where cross-analysis happens — one of the most powerful ideation tools.

  • What are the winning ads? What desires do they target?
  • How do they transfer beliefs? What formats are working?
  • What failed and why?
  • Cross-analysis: Map which desires are being talked to vs. which are missing → go there
  • Look at winners from OTHER ad accounts → adapt for this client
  • Tag each ad by avatar category
  • Where can we add format diversity?

This is a key area for agentic AI — cross-analysis across accounts, identifying desire gaps, and flagging format opportunities can be automated.

Phase 2
Data Extraction & Avatar Building
Turn raw research into a targeting weapon
🏷️
2A — 5-Category Data Extraction
AI-Assisted — Strategist Validates
▼

As you research, tag everything into the 5 avatar categories. Use the validation tests to ensure clean tagging.

The 5 Categories + Validation Tests
  • Desires — Can you say "I want X" or "I need Y"? ✓
  • Experiences — Does it describe a circumstance/event without emotion? ✓
  • Emotions — Does it describe how they FEEL? ✓
  • Behaviors — Does it describe what they DO and how often? ✓
  • Demographics — Is it a static characteristic? ✓ (use sparingly)

Output: Organised extraction lists — all desires, all experiences, all emotions, all behaviors, all demographics. Stored in a Google Sheet with tabs per category.

👤
2B — Avatar Construction
AI-Assisted — Strategist Leads
▼

Build avatars in layers — start broad, then combine categories for specificity.

Step 1: Core Avatars (single category each)

e.g. "Wants better sleep" (desire-based), "Tried melatonin, didn't work" (experience-based), "Feels exhausted" (emotion-based)

Step 2: Sub-Avatars (combine 2–3 categories)

e.g. "Want better sleep + melatonin failed + feels hopeless + still researching" — this is where targeting gets lethal

Step 3: Map Sophistication & Priority
  • How many have tried solutions? What's their awareness level?
  • Prioritise by: market size, purchase intent, specificity, competitive whitespace

Target: 5 core avatars, 8–10 sub-avatars. Every ad concept should be taggable to a specific sub-avatar.

🧠
2C — Strategic Analysis (Answer the 17 Questions)
AI-Assisted — Strategist Leads
▼

Answer every core research question with avatar-specific context. Don't give generic answers — every answer should reference which avatar it applies to.

  • How many avatars? Which are priority and why?
  • Market sophistication per avatar?
  • What are the desires? Dream outcomes? Fears?
  • What are their failed solutions?
  • What beliefs do they hold? How must beliefs be transferred?
  • What are their hesitations?
  • Why is this product better/different? (Per avatar + sophistication level)
  • What is the product's mechanism?
  • What content does the audience like to watch? (Per avatar behavior)
  • How to sell as friend vs. authority? (Per avatar)
  • Are there Greatest Hits that can be applied?

Output: Complete strategy document. Copy/paste into Google Doc. Store in company drive. This is the creative bible for the account.

Phase 3
Ideation & Messaging
3 new concepts per client per week. Message first, format second.
💡
3A — Concept Ideation (Where Do Ideas Come From?)
Strategist — Pattern Recognition
▼

This is the creative engine. Ideas come from multiple sources — the strategist's job is to connect dots.

  • Remake a winner from a different ad account with this client's messaging
  • Remake a viral organic video for the ad account
  • Try a format you haven't done for this client yet
  • New messaging + proven format — find a new angle and put it in a format you know works
  • Same messaging + new format — take what's working and diversify the delivery
  • Cross-analysis gap fill — target a desire the account isn't currently talking to
  • Greatest Hits library — check winning angles and script templates for applicable patterns
  • Aspiration channel — internal inspiration feed of great ads

There's a weekly brainstorming call and a weekly strategy school where this gets discussed. But A-player strategists should be working near-autonomously on this.

✍️
3B — Messaging: The Promise + Proof
Strategist
▼

Messaging is the #1 priority. The format is just the vehicle — the message is what sells. A bad message in a great format will fail. A great message in any format has a shot.

  • Every ad needs a clear promise — what's in it for the audience?
  • Every ad needs proof — why should they believe you?
  • It must target a specific emotion / mindstate
  • It must be aimed at a specific awareness level
  • It must be for a specific avatar / sub-avatar
  • There must be zero confusion — if they don't get it, they won't buy
Awareness Level → Lead Type
  • Unaware: Story lead — make them aware of the problem first
  • Problem Aware: Proclamation lead — "Why the Chinese NEVER get sick!"
  • Solution Aware: Problem-solution lead — start with the problem, dive into the solution
  • Product Aware: Promise lead — "Grow your hair 10x quicker!"
  • Most Aware: Offer lead — "Buy now, 20% OFF!"

Goal: Always try to go UP the awareness levels. Unaware → Solution Aware is where you get the most spend. Most advertising lives at Product Aware — we don't want to be most advertisers.

Emotional Frameworks to Apply
Survival / Life Extension Enjoyment of Food Freedom from Fear & Pain Sexual Companionship Comfortable Living Superiority / Status Protection of Loved Ones Social Approval Autonomy Achievement Belonging Competence Esteem Engagement Empowerment Nurturance Security

Purple = Life Force 8 (Drew Whitman) · Blue = Mindstates (Sarah Levinger / Will Leech)

Phase 4
Hook Engineering
80% of your effort should go on the first 3 seconds
🎯
4A — The Hook Hierarchy
AI Can Draft — Human Refines
▼
0–1 Second: The Hook (Most Important)

Something visually or audibly intriguing. A claim, a movement, a zoom, a WOW factor. Text hook should include a benefit while driving curiosity. Ask: "Who else is putting this in their ads?"

1–3 Seconds: The Reason to Stay

Tell the audience what's in it for them. Give a benefit or tease a payoff. This is where most drop-off happens if you don't deliver.

3–5 Seconds: The Re-Hook

If you've already given the benefit, re-hook them. "Three reasons I'm obsessed with these pants." If you haven't given the benefit, deliver it NOW. The audience must know why this matters to them.

5-Second Self-Check: (1) Will this hook the audience? (2) What promise am I giving? (3) Does the audience know what's in it for them? (4) Any confusion or disconnect? (5) Does it target an emotion?

Hook bank: Organise hooks by avatar. 3+ hooks per priority avatar minimum.

Phase 5
Briefing
The brief changes based on the format. Every format has a template.
📝
5A — UGC / Yapper Ad Brief
AI Can Draft Script — Human Refines
▼

A great UGC brief has a great story, a clear promise, and reads like a real person talking — fillers in, not super polished. Use script templates from Greatest Hits as starting frameworks.

  • Script with exact timings (hook, problem, solution, demo, proof, CTA)
  • Visual direction — what should the creator show at each moment?
  • Tone and energy — casual friend? Authority? Emotional?
  • Specific avatar being targeted
  • The belief transfer that needs to happen
  • Alaina reviews all UGC scripts before they go to creators
🎨
5B — Static Ad Brief
AI Can Own Simple Formats
▼

Write the copy and show what you want it to look like. For simple formats like "3 Reasons Why," AI can handle end to end.

  • Headline / hook copy
  • Body copy with promise + proof
  • Visual direction — layout, imagery, mood
  • CTA
  • Reference examples if available
🎞️
5C — Video / VSL / AI-Gen Brief
AI-Assisted — Strategist Leads
▼
  • VSL: AI voiceover script + b-roll direction + visual cues
  • AI-Gen Video: Concept (Pixar characters, wild visuals, etc.) + messaging overlay + timing
  • Video edits (existing content): Clip selection + edit style + hook + CTA

If content already exists for it, you can go straight to designers. If not, brief creators or use AI generation.

Phase 6
Production
Creators create. Designers design. Editors edit. AI generates.
🎥
6A — Creator / Designer / Editor Execution
Production Team
▼
  • UGC/Yapper: Creator films based on the brief
  • Statics: Designer builds the visual
  • Video edits: Editor assembles from existing clips + b-roll
  • AI-gen: AI generates visuals, voiceover, video

Key principles during production: ads must look native to the platform (not TV-polished), great lighting is a must, cuts every 2–3 seconds (3–5 for 50+ audiences), no dull moments, show the product frequently. Video length should roughly match target age range (20–30 year olds = 20–30 second ads).

Every ad should include (as applicable): Promise, Benefits, Features, Social Proof, Credibility/Authority, Risk Reversals, CTA.

Phase 8
Performance Monitoring
Know your numbers. Context is everything.
📉
8A — Daily / Weekly Performance Review
AI Can Surface Data — Strategist Interprets
▼

Primary metric: Spend at target CPA. Can we scale spend while holding CPA the client is happy with?

Diagnostic metrics:

  • Hook rate — is it grabbing attention?
  • Hold rate — are people staying?
  • CTR — are they clicking?
  • Frequency — is it burning out?
  • Funnel position — top, middle, bottom?
  • Cost per purchase / CPA — the ultimate measure

Look at top performers: last 7 days for newer accounts, last 30 days for established ones. Editors AND strategists should know their numbers. If you made a hook that got 40% hook rate vs. one that got 15%, you need to know that and learn from it.

Phase 9
The Decision Branch
Every ad tells you something. Winning or losing, the data feeds the next move.
⚡ Ad Performance Assessment
Ad Is Winning
  • If creator-led: Send script to new creators — different faces, same message
  • If static: Change the visual — keep the copy, diversify the look
  • New format: Take the core message and put it into a completely different format
  • Video conversion: Can we make this a video? Or vice versa?
  • Hook variations: Test new visual hooks on the same body
  • Scale the messaging — you've found what works. Run the greatest hits playbook on it.
  • → Create 3 iterations per winning concept
Ad Is Losing
  • Check the metrics: Where is it failing? Hook rate? Hold rate? CTR? CPA?
  • Poor hook rate? The hook isn't grabbing attention. Fix the first 1–3 seconds.
  • Good hook, poor hold? It's hooking but not delivering. Is the payoff there?
  • Good engagement, poor CPA? Entertaining but not selling. Is the proof strong enough?
  • Always go back to fundamentals:
  • → Is the desire strong enough?
  • → Is there enough proof?
  • → Is it confusing?
  • → Is the story believable? Does it shift the belief?
  • → Are we too far down the awareness level?
  • Fix and retest — many winners come from fixing first-round losers

Stays Human (The Irreplaceable Bits)
Human Judgment — AI Can't Do This Yet
▼
  • Manual deep research: The Reddit rabbit holes, the TikTok comment mining, the "I found a story" moments — this is where the best ideas come from
  • Pattern recognition across accounts: Seeing a winner in one account and knowing it'll work in another — intuitive, experience-based
  • Messaging decisions: Deciding WHICH desire to pursue, which awareness level to target, which belief to shift — strategic judgment
  • Creative taste: Is this actually good? Does it feel real? Would YOU stop scrolling? The "WOW factor" test
  • Alaina's sign-off: Final quality gate requires experienced human judgment
  • Strategy school & brainstorming: The collaborative ideation that generates breakthrough ideas
  • Client relationships: Understanding what the client wants, managing expectations, communicating strategy
  • Knowing when to break the rules: Every rule has exceptions. Only humans know when.
