The Golden Rules
Every Ad Must Follow These 10 Rules
Memorise these. They are non-negotiable.
1. Target emotions — emotion comes first
2. There must be a promise
3. There must be proof
4. Zero confusion
5. They must sell
6. They must entertain
7. They must be memorable
8. Creativity wins
9. Assumptions kill ads
10. Show the product (first 2 secs)

Phase 2
Data Extraction & Avatars
Step 2A · AI-Assisted
5-Category Data Extraction
Tag all research into: Desires ("I want X"), Experiences (events, no emotion), Emotions (how they feel), Behaviors (what they do), Demographics (static traits, use sparingly). Store in Google Sheet with tabs per category.
Structured5 Categories
Step 2B · AI-Assisted
Avatar Construction
Core Avatars (single category each, 5 minimum) → Sub-Avatars (combine 2–3 categories, 8–10 total) → Map sophistication + priority (market size, purchase intent, competitive whitespace). Every ad concept must tag to a specific sub-avatar.
Core → SubPrioritise
Step 2C · AI-Assisted
Strategic Analysis — Answer the 17 Questions
Every answer must be avatar-specific: How many avatars + priorities? Market sophistication per avatar? Desires, dream outcomes, fears? Failed solutions? Belief transfers needed? Hesitations? Why better/different (per avatar + sophistication)? Product mechanism? Content preferences? Greatest Hits applicable?

Output: Complete strategy document → Google Doc → company drive. The creative bible.
Strategy Bible17 Questions

Step 3B · Human
Messaging: Promise + Proof
Message is #1. Format is just the vehicle. Every ad needs: Clear promise (what's in it for them), Proof (why believe you), specific emotion/mindstate, specific awareness level, specific avatar/sub-avatar, zero confusion.

Goal: Always go UP the awareness levels. Unaware → Solution Aware = most spend. Don't live at Product Aware like everyone else.
PromiseProofEmotion
Awareness Levels → Lead Types
Unaware
Story lead — make them aware of the problem
Problem Aware
Proclamation lead — "Why the Chinese NEVER get sick!"
Solution Aware
Problem-solution lead — best starting point for new accounts
Product Aware
Promise lead — "Grow your hair 10x quicker!"
Most Aware
Offer lead — "Buy now, 20% OFF!"

Step 4 · AI Can Draft
Hook Engineering
0–1s: The hook. Visual or audible intrigue. WOW factor. Text hook = benefit + curiosity.
1–3s: What's in it for them? Benefit or tease payoff.
3–5s: Re-hook. "3 reasons I'm obsessed with these."

80% of effort on first 3 seconds. Build a hook bank per avatar (3+ each).
Hook Bank80/20 Rule

Phase 6
Production
Production · Team Executes
Creator / Designer / Editor Execution

Phase 8
Performance
Step 8 · AI Surfaces Data → Human Interprets
Daily / Weekly Performance Review
Primary: Spend at target CPA.
Diagnostics: Hook rate, Hold rate, CTR, Frequency, Funnel position, Cost per purchase.

Top performers: last 7 days (newer) / 30 days (established). Editors AND strategists must know their numbers. "When I did X, I got 40% hook rate. When I did Y, 15%. Do more of X."
DailyKnow Your Numbers
⚡ Phase 9: The Decision Branch
Ad Is Winning
  • Creator-led: Send script to new creators
  • Static: Change visual, keep copy
  • New format: Same message, different vehicle
  • Video conversion: Static → video or vice versa
  • Hook variations: New hooks on same body
  • Scale the messaging → 3 iterations per winner
Ad Is Losing
  • Poor hook rate? Fix first 1–3 seconds
  • Good hook, poor hold? Payoff missing
  • Good engagement, poor CPA? Not selling
  • → Is the desire strong enough?
  • → Is there enough proof?
  • → Is it confusing?
  • → Is the story believable?
  • → Too far down awareness level?
  • Fix and retest — many winners come from fixing losers
